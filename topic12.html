Topic 2 Economic methodology

Economics as a social science is primarily divided into two areas of study, these areas are known to be Microeconomics that analyses an isolated perspective on singular goods or services as individual markets and Macroeconomics that examines the broader economics of a group of economies i.e regions, nations, unions.Within these distinctions, there may also exist further division of factor markets where land, labour and capital are bought and sold and product market where the same is true for finished goods and services.

These classifications are primarily done through the use of models, an idealised hypothetical version of a possible scenario in economics made as simple as possible. This is primarily done through the use of assumptions and constants. “Ceteris paribus” is the accurate term to describe how economics studies the change of one variable while other factors remain constant.

Like any other field of study, there is both objectivity and subjectivity within economics. If a statement is said to be objective, testable and based on evidence or can be dispelled by testing or use of evidence it is considered to be a positive statement. Likewise, a statement that resembles an opinion, a take that stems from assumptions rather than universally accepted truths or facts is said to be a normative statement.

A crucial element of how economics studies change is based on the constant element of time. In Economics, there exist 4 time periods that determine whether or not crucial factors of productions can vary and aid in production.

These four time periods are:

  • Very short run (L,La,K,E) Fixed.
  • Short run (La) Variable; (L,K,E) Fixed.
  • Long run (L,La,K) Variable (E) Fixed
  • Very long run (L,La,K,E) Variable
